Across years of rigorous academics, hands-on research, organizational leadership, and community-driven initiatives, I have grown into a chemical engineer in the making—one who leads with purpose, adapts with resilience, and designs with a conscience.

I’ve led national events, managed cross-functional teams of over 100 people, and represented the College of Engineering in both technical and cultural pursuits. From pioneering virtual student programs during the pandemic to coordinating large-scale collaborative projects like Talanghay and NACHES, I’ve learned to lead not just with strategy, but with heart. As a volunteer, I’ve continuously placed service at the core of my identity—whether through Red Cross missions, mentorship of freshies, or facilitating access to career development opportunities for my peers.

My commitment to sustainability is evident in the choices I make—from designing bio-based chemical processes to championing renewable energy advocacy in national platforms. With every project, I aim to balance innovation with impact, performance with purpose. My portfolio shows not only my technical proficiency in design, process optimization, and safety, but also my versatility in bridging science with service, systems with people, and engineering with empathy.

Above all, I see chemical engineering as more than a profession—it is a lifelong mission to lead responsibly, build sustainably, and serve meaningfully.
